  • Feed Grade Slow-release Urea
    Chemical formula: CH4N2O
Product Description

Feed Grade slow-release urea is a feed grade urea that is encapsulated by fats and oils. It can be released slowly in the rumen of cattle and sheep for a long time. It can effectively maintain the rumen ammonia concentration compared with normal feed grade urea. It can improve the utilization of nitrogen in animals, improve the efficiency of microbial protein synthesis, promote rumen fermentation, improve animal growth performance, reduce the metabolic emission of ammonia in farms, and reduce environmental pollution.

Product Specification
Caution

1. The amount of urea should be added gradually from less to more, and it is recommended to have a transition adaptation period of 2 weeks.

2. Urea must be mixed with feed and fed to cattle. Never feed urea to cattle alone or directly dissolved in water for cattle to drink.

3. It is not recommended to add urea when the crude protein content provided by natural feeds in the whole mixed feed for cattle and sheep is less than 9% or more than 12%.
